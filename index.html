Nick Drake

An Accomplished Acoustic Guitarist and Melancholic Songwriter

A Timeless talent

➤ Nick Drake, full name Nicholas Rodney Drake, was born on June 19, 1948, in Rangoon, Burma (now Yangon, Myanmar).
➤ He was an English singer-songwriter known for his distinctive guitar-based compositions and melancholy voice.
➤ Drake released three albums during his lifetime: "Five Leaves Left" (1969), "Bryter Layter" (1970), and "Pink Moon" (1972).
➤ Despite his talent, Nick Drake's albums did not sell well during his lifetime, leading to a struggle with depression.
➤ His work gained significant posthumous recognition, and he is now considered one of the most influential artists in folk and acoustic music.
➤ Nick Drake's music has been cited as an influence by many contemporary artists, including Robert Smith of The Cure and Peter Buck of R.E.M.
➤ Nick Drake passed away on November 25, 1974, at the age of 26, due to an overdose of prescribed antidepressants.
➤ His legacy lives on through his music, which continues to inspire and resonate with new generations of listeners.
